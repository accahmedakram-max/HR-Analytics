# HR Analytics & Employee Performance Dashboard

## Project Overview
This interactive HR Dashboard provides a comprehensive analysis of workforce demographics, employee performance, and salary distribution. It is designed to help HR departments identify trends in employee retention, diversity, and compensation across various departments and education levels.

## Dashboard Visualizations & Insights

### 1. Employee Retention & Attrition
* [cite_start]**Attrition by Gender:** The dashboard tracks attrition volume by gender, showing 150 male employees and 87 female employees have left the company[cite: 1].
* [cite_start]**Departmental Impact:** Attrition is broken down by department, with **R&D** (133) having the highest count, followed by **Sales** (92) and **HR** (12)[cite: 1].
* [cite_start]**Education Field:** The highest attrition occurs among employees with a background in **Life Sciences** (89) and **Medical** (63)[cite: 1].

### 2. Salary & Compensation Analysis
* **Salary Slaps:** Employees are categorized by salary ranges. [cite_start]The largest group of employees (409) earns up to $5k, while the smallest group (124) earns over $15k[cite: 1].
* [cite_start]**Average Salary by Department:** * **Sales:** $6.96K [cite: 1]

### 3. Demographic & Career Distribution
* [cite_start]**Age Distribution:** A histogram shows the workforce volume across age bins, highlighting that the largest concentration of employees is between the ages of 31 and 40[cite: 1].
* [cite_start]**Job Roles:** The dashboard lists employee counts by specific roles, with **Sales Executives** (326) and **Research Scientists** (292) being the most prominent[cite: 1].
* [cite_start]**Education Level:** Employees are segmented by education level, ranging from High School to Doctorate[cite: 1].

### 4. Employee Satisfaction & Performance
* [cite_start]**Job Satisfaction:** A matrix categorizes employees based on their roles and satisfaction levels (1 to 4), identifying that **Laboratory Technicians** and **Sales Executives** report high counts of satisfaction[cite: 1].
